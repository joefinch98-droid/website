Commercial brick manufacturing commences with raw material procurement, wherein clay, shale, and sand aggregates are excavated and pulverized in primary crushers to achieve uniform particle size distribution below 50 mm. Subsequent pug milling incorporates water and additives like plasticizers to form a cohesive pug, extruded through a de-aired vacuum press at 200-500 psi into columnar slugs. Wire-cut machinery slices slugs into green bricks, which undergo mechanical setting on kiln cars. Firing in tunnel kilns at 900-1200Â°C induces vitrification via solid-state diffusion and flux-mediated sintering, yielding compressive strengths exceeding 10 MPa per ASTM C62 standards. Post-cooling, bricks are cubically tumbled for surface texture, inspected for efflorescence, and palletized for dispatch.
